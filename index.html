About Me


Hello everyone! My name is Ricky Mavlingcar. I am an international student, currently in my senior year studying Information Technology and Systems at the University of Texas at Dallas. I am Portuguese by nationality but was born in Goa, India and raised in Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates. My obsession with technology started at an early age. My father owns an I.T. company that offers consultancy, installation and procurement services in the U.A.E. and would always try to teach me about what he and his company did. Over time, I would get more curious and ask more detailed questions related to computing to the point where he simply decided that I needed a computer to go and find answers on my own time. I am passionate about so many topics in my field that I don't even know where to begin! I love learning and tinkering with new technology, especially if I can utilise them in my current projects. My strengths include being a persistent and stubborn developer, and a learner.

I've dabbled in multiple areas of IT, but if I were to quickly state my favourite topics, it would be as follows: Database Management, Responsive Web Design and Big Data Analytics. I plan on working once I graduate and am actively seeking opportunities where I can demonstrate my skills and learn. I lean more towards the technical side of I.T. since I have a love for both scripting and development. Because of this, I am looking for positions that allow me to work with databases or as a developer. To look at some of my work, scroll down to the projects section.

Table Tennis


 

I love this sport! I've been hooked since I was first introduced to it in sixth grade. My style of play has definitely evolved over time. When starting out, I would tend to be more aggressive and loop constantly in order to apply pressure and win a point. Over time, I got more reserved with my loops in an attempt to play more efficiently. I would classify my current play style to be most similar to modern defense, which basically means that I will naturally try to bait the opponent into conceding a point by defending and waiting for an opening to capitalise on.

Nowadays, in addition to playing recreationally, I also enjoy playing table tennis for the UT Dallas table tennis club and have taken part in various tournaments while studying full time. I consider myself to be a somewhat casual player since I play the game more for fun than anything else. I'm willing to play against anyone who's up for it. You can usually find me playing table tennis on Thursday's at the table tennis club. If you would like to play some table tennis and are a student at the University of Texas at Dallas, I encourage you to come to our club meetings. Everyone is welcome, regardless of skill level. If you are looking to learn, the club is a great resource and we will critique your form and help you practice.

UT Dallas Table Tennis Club Timings: Monday's and Thursday's from 7:00pm to 10:00pm at the Auxilliary Gym located in the Activities Centre.

Projects

HIIT Me Up - Mobile Web Application Development - Spring 2016

HIIT Me Up is a workout management mobile web app that my group and I designed as part of a semester long group project for Mobile Web Application Development. The app was designed using HTML5, CSS3, jQuery, PHP and the jQuery Mobile Framework. We used XAMPP as the testing environment. This project was challenging as it was my first proper mobile web application project and we were expected to learn about CSS, PHP and jQuery while designing a mobile web app over the semester. I created the database and mock data by creating a script in SQL, created the forms for user input and navigation bars, and worked on all back-end scripting that involved connecting with our database.

If you want to learn more about this project, you can view our final presentation slides here or contact me by submitting a form on the contact page. I'd be happy to answer any of your questions. The slides show the screenshots of our app on a mobile device. While the app could still use a lot of work, it was a project that allowed us to learn and apply basic mobile web design elements that are necessary for a successful mobile web app.

Diverging Diamond Intersection(DDI) Prototype - Sponsored by AT&T - ITSS Capstone - Fall 2016

The DDI Prototype is a web app designed for my capstone project. AT&T have partnered with the Texas Department of Transportation and have tasked this year's capstone class with creating an application that is able to utilise traffic data provided by the Texas Department of Transportation (TxDOT) and generate a list of candidate intersections that would benefit from implementing the DDI model.

This project is the hardest one I've ever attempted to complete. It presented some unique challenges and introduced me to many new ideas and methodologies. The class was split into four groups of 7 members and given the semester to design a working prototype. There was limited oversight and each group was only given a small dataset and a project scope. We were left to determine the best way to implement and realise the project goals given to us.

We designed the prototype based on the skills of each individual member of the group and decided that our prototype would be a web application. The prototype was designed using HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, jQuery, Google Sheets, Google Fusion Tables and Google Charts.

To learn more about this project, you can view the final presentation slides that we used when detailing our prototype to AT&T here.
